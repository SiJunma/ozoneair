Questions and answers about ozone generator

Ozone is a naturally occurring variant of oxygen. It is extremely reactive. Ozone binds and neutralizes odors.
In addition, it destroys bacteria, viruses and mold spores.

Ozoneair uses ozone. A chemical reaction occurs when the ozone encounters the molecules that cause bad odors. It neutralizes the bad odor. In addition, the ozone destroys the cell walls of bacteria and mold to stop them from reproducing. However, it’s always important to investigate and remedy the actual source of the problem.

The gas ozone can be produced in different ways. It’s important to pay attention to the way in which ozone is produced in the ozone generator. While some generators are efficient, they cause more residual products to be produced, and these can be hazardous to your health.

Different methods of producing ozone:

Ultraviolet light

UV light with a specific wavelength can be used to produce ozone. However, these levels of ozone are very low, and they generally only work if they are supplied continuously. This method works best in garbage disposal rooms, public bathrooms, and other places you only stay in for a short period of time.

+ Good for continuous air purification.
+ Does not cause harmful residual products.
– The levels of ozone are not high enough to have an effect on bacteria, mold spores, viruses and allergens, or severe odors that have settled in surfaces and materials.

Spreading the right amount of ozone during the right amount of time is the key to a good result. It is also crucial to avoid damaging plastic, rubber, electronics, and other materials.

A conventional ozone generator spreads the highest amount of ozone closest to the product itself and only operates for a short time. The high ozone concentration around the appliance results in a greater risk of damaging materials. In addition, you often have to repeat the treatment as many odor problems require treatment for more than just a short time.
